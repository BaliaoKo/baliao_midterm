Discover Surigao del Norte

Surigao del Norte is a province located in the northeastern part of Mindanao, Philippines. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, pristine beaches, and friendly locals, Surigao del Norte offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and a relaxed atmosphere that makes it a perfect destination for travelers and a wonderful place to call home.

The province is home to the famous Siargao Island, widely regarded as the surfing capital of the Philippines, attracting adventurers and surfers from around the world. But Surigao del Norte has much more to offer, from crystal-clear lagoons, waterfalls, and hidden caves to vibrant festivals and delicious local delicacies.

Popular Tourist Spots in Surigao del Norte

Siargao Island

Famous for its surfing, particularly Cloud 9, Siargao is a paradise for beach lovers and adventure seekers. The island also offers beautiful lagoons, mangrove forests, and is famous for its rock pools and island-hopping tours.

Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park

This national park features impressive limestone formations, rivers, and caves. Visitors can enjoy activities like kayaking, swimming, and exploring the park’s natural beauty.

Naked Island

An uninhabited island with soft white sand and clear blue waters, Naked Island is perfect for sunbathing and relaxing. It’s often included in island-hopping tours from Siargao.

Daku Island

Known for its picturesque beaches and laid-back atmosphere, Daku Island is a great spot for swimming, snorkeling, and enjoying fresh seafood.

Magpupungko Rock Pools

Located in Pilar, these natural rock pools are accessible during low tide and offer great swimming spots amidst beautiful scenery.

Timoga Springs

While technically in the nearby province of Lanao del Norte, this site often attracts visitors from Surigao. It features natural spring pools and is ideal for a refreshing swim.

Bucas Grande Island

Known for its stunning lagoons and the famous Sohoton Cove, Bucas Grande is great for water activities like kayaking and snorkeling.

Crooked Island

A lesser-known gem, this island features stunning beaches and is perfect for a day of relaxation away from the crowds.

Cagwait Beach

Located in the town of Cagwait, this beach is known for its crescent shape and white sand. It’s an excellent spot for swimming and sunbathing.

Surigao City

The capital city itself has attractions such as the Surigao City Cathedral, the historic Mabua Pebble Beach, and the picturesque San Juanico Bridge connecting to Leyte.

History of Surigao del Norte

Pre-Colonial Period

Before the arrival of the Spaniards, the area now known as Surigao del Norte was populated by various indigenous groups with their own distinct cultures and practices. The region was rich in resources, and its coastal areas supported fishing and trade with neighboring islands and territories.

Spanish Colonization (16th Century)

Surigao was among the first areas in Mindanao to be visited by Spanish explorers. In 1565, Miguel López de Legazpi arrived in the area and claimed it for Spain. The town of Surigao was established, and it became a significant port and center of trade during the Spanish colonial period. The discovery of gold and other mineral resources attracted settlers and Spanish authorities.

The Spanish introduced Christianity, and many indigenous people converted to Catholicism. Various missions were established, and the influence of the Catholic Church deepened in the region.

American Period (Late 19th - Early 20th Century)

In 1898, the Philippines was ceded to the United States after the Spanish-American War. The American colonial government implemented changes in governance and education, introducing a new system of civil administration.

Surigao became the capital of the province of Surigao, which then encompassed both Surigao del Norte and Surigao del Sur. The province was officially divided into two separate provinces in 1970.

World War II (1941-1945)

During World War II, Surigao was invaded by Japanese forces. The area saw various military activities, and the local population experienced significant hardship. The liberation of the Philippines began in 1944, and American forces retook Surigao and surrounding areas.

Post-Independence Era

After the Philippines gained independence in 1946, Surigao del Norte continued to develop, with agriculture, fishing, and mining forming the backbone of its economy. Infrastructure improvements, such as roads and schools, were made in the years following independence. The province became known for its natural beauty, featuring beaches, mountain ranges, and rich marine biodiversity.

Recent Developments

In recent decades, Surigao del Norte has gained recognition for its tourism potential, particularly in areas like Siargao Island, famous for its surfing spots. The local government and communities have increasingly focused on sustainable tourism to promote economic development while preserving the environment.

The region has faced challenges, including natural disasters like typhoons and earthquakes. Significant events, such as the 2017 earthquake, have prompted increased awareness and strengthening of disaster response and preparedness measures in the province.

Festivals in Surigao del Norte

1. Bonok-Bonok Maradong Festival

Location: Surigao City

Date: Celebrated annually in March, typically around the feast day of St. Joseph, the city’s patron saint.

Description: This vibrant festival features street dancing, cultural presentations, and a series of activities showcasing the history, culture, and talents of Surigao City. The festival is named after the local term "bonok-bonok," which refers to the unique rhythm of the dance performed during the festivities.

2. Siargao Surfing Festival

Location: Siargao Island

Date: Usually held during the month of September.

Description: This festival celebrates Siargao’s reputation as one of the premier surfing destinations in the world. It includes various surfing competitions, beach activities, live music, and a showcase of local arts and crafts.

3. Higaonon Festival

Location: The town of Bacuag

Date: Celebrated in February or March.

Description: This festival honors the indigenous Higaonon people and showcases their culture through traditional dances, music, and rituals. The festival aims to promote the cultural heritage of the Higaonon community.

4. Kawhi Festival

Location: The municipality of Claver

Date: Celebrated in June.

Description: This festival highlights the local coconut industry, with various activities that showcase the versatility of coconut products. It features street dancing, a trade fair, and the showcasing of local crafts and delicacies.

5. Linggo ng Parole Festival

Location: The municipality of Tandag (note: sometimes also associated with Surigao del Norte)

Date: Usually held in December.

Description: While this festival often takes place in December, it is celebrated near the Christmas season. The festival focuses on traditional practices, crafts, and local culture, often highlighted by colorful parol (Christmas lantern) displays and contests.

6. Saging Festival

Location: The municipality of Malimono

Date: Typically held in the month of October.

Description: This festival celebrates the abundance of bananas (saging) in the area, showcasing various banana-based dishes and products. Activities often include fun games, a parade, and cultural presentations.

7. Daragang Magayon Festival

Location: Various municipalities in the province

Date: Celebrated in May.

Description: This festival is meant to promote local culture and tourism, featuring a range of cultural performances, street dancing, and beauty pageants.
